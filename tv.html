Music Visualizers

January 1, 2014 by Matt

Back in high school I read you could cut the signal leads for a CRT, wire them up to a 3.5mm input jack, and make it a signal visualizer. Later on I explored microcontroller based projects with a great degree of success.

CRT Efforts

CRT televisions have two different refresh rates:a vertical rate of 60hz and a horizontal one of several kHz which varies between screen sizes.

Propellor-Based Music Visualizer

In college I read about a project called the PixelMusic 3000, based upon the unsuccessful and very 70's Atari Video Music. I ordered the

I wanted to incorporate lights and UI into one unit. As i was working on restoring a vintage arcade machine at the time, I picked up some very cool looking Ultralux Chrome Illuminated Pushbuttons from This arcade supply store.

I chose the specific color buttons you can see here due to them most closely resembling the original panel of the manager.

Finished Product and Potential Expansions
