Music Visualizers

January 1, 2014 by Matt

Back in high school I read you could cut the signal leads for a CRT, wire them up to a 3.5mm input jack, and make it a signal visualizer. Later on I explored microcontroller based projects with a great degree of success.

CRT Efforts

CRT televisions have two different refresh rates:a vertical rate of 60hz and a horizontal one of several kHz which varies between screen sizes.

Propellor-Based Music Visualizer

In college I read about a project called the PixelMusic 3000, based upon the unsuccessful and very 70's Atari Video Music. I ordered the parts, sketched up a schematic, and it works pretty well!

Finished Product and Potential Expansions

I ended up moving before I was able to completely finish the project, but the demo video above available here shows it working good as gold. I had ordered PCBs, and a (wood-paneled!) enclosure. Another potential improvment would be to have 3.5mm to Composite adapters instead of hard wiring the cables to the enclosure, to give it a cleaner look.
